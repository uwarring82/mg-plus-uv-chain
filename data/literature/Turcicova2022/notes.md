**Citation:** H. Turcicova, O. Novak, J. Muzik, D. Stepankova, M. Smrz, and T. Mocek, "Laser induced damage threshold (LIDT) of β-barium borate (BBO) and cesium lithium borate (CLBO) – Overview," *Optics & Laser Technology* **149**, 107876 (2022), DOI: `10.1016/j.optlastec.2022.107876`.

## Why this paper

Turcicova2022 is the most recent published BBO+CLBO LIDT review and the bibliographic hub identified by the task-E scout pass. It was extracted in this round to (i) bracket the 280 nm CW LIDT question against the published pulse-duration scaling, (ii) harvest its reference list for primary sources the scout passes missed, and (iii) capture its discussion of intrinsic vs extrinsic damage mechanisms, AR-coating LIDT lowering, and SFG / FiHG operating envelopes.

It is **secondary literature** (`source_tier: S`). The Section C `[P]`-only rule applies: Turcicova2022 cannot be the sole citation for a `KD-UV280-005` constraint. Its role is to point the steward at the right primary sources and to provide the qualitative scaffold (mechanism families, AR-coating dependence, SFG cross-action) for later primary extractions.

## Extraction Scope

The 2026-05-06 pass extracts:

- §1 introduction: BBO and CLBO physical-property comparison (transparency edge, d_eff, walk-off at FHG, angular acceptance, hygroscopicity) and the TPA discussion (2-photon absorption coefficients at 266 nm and 248 nm in both polarisations; temperature-dependent TPA reduction).
- §2 LIDT-vs-pulse-duration compendium (Figs. 1a/b for BBO, 2a/b for CLBO), with explicit identification of the single CW data point in Fig. 1b that anchors the only published CW BBO LIDT in the dataset. Numerical bracket of the BBO LIDT scatter across 10 ps - 10 ns pulse durations.
- §2 wavelength dependence (BBO LIDT at DUV is lower than at visible / NIR) and crystal-direction dependence (factor of 2 between {001} and {100}/{010} planes for 1064 nm). Crystallinity-vs-LIDT correlation.
- §3 measurement-technique distinction (1-on-1 vs S-on-1 vs rasterscan, ISO 21254). Practically, S-on-1 LIDT can be ~2x lower than 1-on-1 LIDT; data-sheet vendor numbers without S-on-1 disclosure should be treated as upper bounds.
- §4 intrinsic vs extrinsic damage mechanisms (electrostriction, hyper-sound, electron impact ionisation, photoionisation; absorbing inclusions, surface-polishing residuals, sub-micron polishing-material inclusions). The thermal-explosion-via-inclusions model (Manenkov 2014) is cited as the canonical theoretical reference.
- §4.1 colour-centre / dynamic-defect mechanism: lifetime 1-100 ms, bleaches between pulses at <100 Hz, accumulates at high repetition rate, density reduced by ~69 % at 200 °C in BBO (cite Kumar2015 / Takahashi2010).
- §4.2 LIDT at SFG / FiHG: the HiLASE rule of thumb (sum of 4H + 1H peak intensity < 7 GW / cm² for ~1-2 ps pulses, larger crystal apertures). Direct observation of BBO crystal cracking at 3.5 GW / cm² (4H) + 1.8 GW / cm² (1H) simultaneous pumping. The mutual-acting-1H+4H question is identified as theoretically unresolved.
- §4.3 AR-coating LIDT lowering: HfO₂ + Al₂O₃ + SiO₂ multilayer for BBO @ 532 + 266 nm, LIDT ~1 GW / cm² (sample likely not premium grade; AR @ 266 nm-only ~20 % lower than AR @ 532 nm-only). Ion-beam-sputtering (IBS) > magnetron / e-beam / pulsed-laser-deposition for damage tolerance. Mixture coatings (ZrO₂-SiO₂) > discrete Al₂O₃ / SiO₂ at 355 nm. Multi-wavelength AR-coatings substantially reduce LIDT vs single-wavelength coatings.

## Review Notes

- **The single CW data point.** Fig. 1b for BBO contains exactly one CW data point in the lower-right corner, at LIDT ≈ 10⁻³-10⁻⁴ GW / cm² (i.e. roughly 1-10 kW / cm²). The numerical value is not given in the body text; the source is identified in the figure's bibliographic chain as the Nikogosyan 2005 *Complete Survey* (ref [6]). The Sony / Cz-BBO Kondo1998 + Kubota1998 result (260 W / cm² average UV power density at 100 mW UV output, with cavity-loss-increase rate 5 × 10⁻⁵ % / h) is **not** the same kind of "LIDT" — it is an operational-lifetime measurement, not a damage-threshold measurement. The Nikogosyan compendium CW data point is likely a separate measurement; the steward should follow the Nikogosyan reference chain to identify it before importing Turcicova's CW point into `KD-UV280-005`.
- **Vendor-catalogue caveat.** §5 conclusion: "It is unclear whether or not the producers always present their own measured LIDT values or if they simply take those over from other producers." This explicit statement justifies treating CASTECH / Altechna / Bluebean Optical / Edmund Optics / MTOptics / Raicol / United Crystals data-sheet LIDT values as `[S]` at best, often `[V]` (vendor), and never as the sole anchor for a Section C constraint.
- **DUV-vs-visible-vs-NIR ordering for BBO.** BBO LIDT at DUV is lower than at visible / NIR. Anchored explicitly in §2 ("the damage threshold for BBO is lower if the incident radiation is UV-DUV") and in Bhar2000 (ref [39]; the comparison shows BBO LIDT at 532 nm < at 1064 nm). For `KD-UV280-005` this is the wavelength-scaling argument that prevents porting 1064 nm or 532 nm vendor LIDT numbers into a 280 nm constraint.
- **CW vs pulsed regime split is large.** The figures span 10⁻⁴ - 10³ GW / cm² (10 orders of magnitude) over pulse durations 10 fs - 10 ms - CW. The CW limit is many orders of magnitude below the ns-pulsed limit, consistent with the thermal / colour-centre mechanism being dominant in CW and the multiphoton mechanism being dominant for short pulses. This is the qualitative scaffold for the `KD-UV280-011` mechanism statement (cf. the Kubota1998 thermal → multiphoton transition argument).
- **Surface vs bulk LIDT distinction.** §2 last paragraph: bulk LIDT is what matters for tightly focused harmonic-generation experiments; surface LIDT is what matters for collimated-beam optics. Surface LIDT is dominated by polishing residuals, sub-micron polishing-material inclusions, and residual scratches that intensify the local optical-electric field. CLBO surface LIDT can be improved 1.4 × by ion-beam etching after polishing (Kamimura2002 / ref [15]). For BBO the same protocol is recommended but no numerical value is given in this review.
- **HiLASE FiHG 7 GW / cm² envelope.** §4.2 closes with the HiLASE operational rule: keep the sum of 4H + 1H peak intensities below 7 GW / cm² for 1-2 ps pulses, using larger crystal apertures. This was learned the hard way from a high-quality CLBO crystal cracking at 4H = 2 GW / cm² + 1H = 11 GW / cm². Directly applicable to FiHG / SFG architectures, not Friedenauer-class CW-doubling architectures.
- **AR-coating LIDT for BBO at 532 + 266 nm.** §4.3 reports HfO₂ + Al₂O₃ + SiO₂ multilayer AR coating for FHG (i.e., 532 nm + 266 nm dual-wavelength) gave LIDT ≈ 1 GW / cm² in the worst direction, likely on a non-premium crystal. The dual-wavelength AR was the same as the AR-@-266 nm-only LIDT and ~20 % lower than the AR-@-532 nm-only LIDT. For the Friedenauer-class 559 + 280 nm dual-wavelength AR, this scaling gives an order-of-magnitude bracket but no direct number; the Steinert2024 thesis (286 nm) is the closer-wavelength precedent.
- **Bibliographic-hub harvest.** Several primary sources cited in Turcicova2022 should be considered for the next round of extraction:
  - **Yoshida2006 (ref [19]):** BBO LIDT 20 % lower than CLBO; BBO {001} LIDT 2 × {100} / {010} at 1064 nm. Wavelength-scaling caveat: the 2 × directional anisotropy was not measured at 532 nm or 257 nm.
  - **Nishioka2005 (ref [20]):** Solution-stirring TSSG-grown CLBO with LIDT 24 GW / cm² (266 nm, 7 ns, 1-on-1) and superior water resistance. Already in the task-E scout list.
  - **Kumar2015 (ref [27]):** TPA 3.5 × lower at 200 °C in BBO; colour-centre density reduced 69 %. PDF retrieved (`Kumar2015_UPCommons_open.pdf`); promote to its own folder.
  - **Bhar2000 (ref [39]):** Surface-damage thresholds in BBO, impurity-LIDT correlation. Already in the task-E scout list.
  - **Manenkov2014 (ref [52]):** Canonical theoretical review of laser-induced damage mechanisms. `[S]`-tier theory anchor.
  - **Takahashi2010 (ref [26]):** TPA at 262 nm in BBO; pulse-rate and temperature dependence. Already in the task-E scout list.
  - **Takachiho2014 (ref [35]):** UV-induced degradation of CLBO and BBO. The Optica metadata probe in `downloads/literature/task-e/Takachiho2014_Optica_captcha_probe.html` failed; PDF still pending.
  - **Kamimura2001/2002/2005 (refs [14,15,31]):** CLBO crystallinity-vs-LIDT and surface ion-etching protocol. Of secondary value to a BBO-at-280-nm task; not promoted to its own folder unless `KD-UV280-006` (CLBO comparison) is reactivated.
- **Negative-result reinforcement.** Turcicova2022 confirms what the scout passes already concluded: there is no peer-reviewed BBO-at-280-nm CW operational-lifetime measurement in the published LIDT review literature. The closest CW number remains the Nikogosyan-compendium CW point (likely Sony Cz-BBO at 266 nm, i.e. Kondo1998 / Kubota1998).
