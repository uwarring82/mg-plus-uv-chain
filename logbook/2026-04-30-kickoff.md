## Open items at v1.0 — flagged for resolution outside the Charter

### Constitution-level (Constitution v0.4 open item #2)

The Council-3 ADM-EC Constitution v0.4 has a known open item: a *fast-path protocol for high-tempo cases* has not yet been designed. The internal Architect (v0.4 cycle), peer review (v0.3 cycle), and Integrator (v0.8 probe) all flagged that the standard Chart-Transition Protocol may be too heavy for routine experimental tactics. CHARTER §9 carves out routine tactics from Council-3 deliberation (with the §9 reclassification rule and §9 logbook trigger question as audit mechanisms) — but a fully designed fast-path protocol remains a Constitution-level open item.

This is *not* a Charter-level item. It is held at the Constitution level and addressed outside this repository when convenient. The Charter ships v1.0 with this open item flagged here, per the v1.0 Steward decision.

### Phase 1 evidence-table refinement (G3 from external review)

The external review (v0.7 → v0.8 cycle) recommended elevating crystal-choice evaluation from Phase 4 to a new Phase 1.5. Architect declined to reopen the phase architecture and instead absorbed the recommendation as an expansion of the Phase 1 literature dossier deliverable: a dedicated crystal/coating evidence table covering BBO, CLBO, LBO-relevant stages, coating degradation, hygroscopic / environmental constraints, and vendor / batch sensitivity. The expanded deliverable is recorded in CHARTER §5 Phase 1 row.

### Generic-template scope expansion (post-v1.0)

Both internal Architect (v0.4) and external Architect (v0.8 stress test) noted that the constraint hierarchy and kill-gate structure could generalise beyond ²⁵Mg⁺ — to Ca⁺, Sr⁺, Yb⁺, molecular ions, and possibly other UV nonlinear-system design problems. Held as a potential v1.x → v2.0 transition if v1.0 ships clean. Not a v1.0 concern; not a Charter open item.
